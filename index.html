My Visual Piano

People with physical disabilities, such as ALS, have trouble expressing their creative abilities with music, especially in live performance. How can we provide a way for users to express their creativity with just their eyes, to allow them to continue their passion?

Why care?

" Every day, an average of 15 people are newly diagnosed with ALS — more than 5,600 people per year. As many as 30,000 Americans may currently be affected by ALS. Annually, ALS is responsible for two deaths per 100,000 people. "
- alsa.org

Learn more

Solution

A live performance piano software that allows users to perform piano music with gesture-based eye tracking. By moving away from a button, or precision-based interface:

  • Rate of intentional inputs will increase, mistakes will decrease
  • Users will be overall less frustrated with their experience
  • Allow for real-time performances

Our Journey

We came together as a team with the intention of creating a product that combined our passions for technology and music. All of us share a hobby in performing music – a creative channel to express ourselves. We believed that every musician should have the ability to tap into their talent, no matter their circumstances, and so Team Dubstep was formed with this in mind.

Technologies

Tobii Eyetracker

Peripheral eye tracker compatible with Windows 10 Fall Creators Update and Windows Gaze Input APIs

Windows 10

Native support for eye tracking in the Windows 10 Fall Creators Update

Gaze Interaction Library

Library built on top of Windows Gaze Input APIs to help track user eye input

Universal Windows Platform

Platform used to develop universal apps on Windows 10
